Latitude and Longitude

Uses of Longitude

i. Determines local time (every 15° = 1 hour difference).

ii. Helps determine the exact position of places with latitude.

iii. Crucial in navigation, mapping, and aviation.

iv. Helps in determining international time zones.


Latitude and Longitude Combined (Coordinates)

The coordinates of a place (exact location on the globe) is determined by giving: Latitude first then Longitude. This is known as the geographical coordinates of a place.

Example: Lagos = 6.5°N, 3.4°E

How Coordinates Work

i. Latitude shows how far north or south.

ii. Longitude shows how far east or west.

Together, they form a grid reference that identifies any point on Earth.


Relationship Between Longitude and Time

i. The Earth rotates 360° in 24 hours.

ii. This means it rotates 15° every hour (360 ÷ 24 = 15).

iii. For every 15° longitude, there is a difference of 1 hour.

iv. Places east of Greenwich are ahead in time (+ time).

v. Places west are behind in time (- time).


Example:

If it is 12 noon at 0°, then at 15°E, it is 1:00 PM.

At 15°W, it is 11:00 AM.


Effects of Latitude on Climate - Latitude strongly influences climate.

i. Equatorial regions (0°): Hot and wet

ii. Tropics (23½°N–23½°S): Very warm

iii. Temperate regions (23½°–66½°): Moderate climate

iv. Polar regions (66½°–90°): Very cold

The amount of sunlight received decreases with distance from the Equator.

Practical Applications

i. GPS technology uses latitude + longitude to identify locations.

ii. Used in mapping, geology, aviation, shipping, and military operations.

iii. Helps in disaster response, search and rescue, and weather forecasting.


CALCULATION ASPECTS OF LATITUDE AND LONGITUDE (TIME & LOCATION)

Geographers use mathematical relationships between the Earth’s rotation, latitude, and longitude to calculate time differences, distance, and exact positions.


Three Most Important rules for solving all longitude–time questions.

i. Earth rotates 360° in 24 hours

ii. 1 hour = 15° of longitude

iii. 1° of longitude = 4 minutes (Because 60 minutes ÷ 15 = 4 minutes)


EXAMPLE 1: Simple longitude time difference

What is the time difference between 30°E and 45°E?

Step 1: Find longitude difference (45°E - 30°E = 15°)

Step 2: Convert degrees to time (15° = 1 hour)

Time difference = 1 hour (45°E is ahead).


EXAMPLE 2: Time at another longitude

If it is 12:00 noon at 0° (Greenwich), what is the time at 45°E?

Step 1: Difference = 45°

Step 2: Convert to time:

45° ÷ 15 = 3 hours

Step 3: Since East → Add

12: 00 + 3 = 3:00 PM

Time at 45°E = 3:00 PM


EXAMPLE 3: West longitude

Time at 60°W when it is 6:00 PM at Greenwich?

Difference = 60°

60 ÷ 15 = 4 hours

West → Subtract

6:00 PM − 4 = 2:00 PM

Time at 60°W = 2:00 PM


EXAMPLE 4: Crossing East and West

When it is 10:00 AM at 40°W, what is the time at 20°E?

Step 1: Add longitudes (because opposite sides)

40°W + 20°E = 60°

Step 2: Convert to time

60° ÷ 15 = 4 hours

Step 3: Move from West → East (+ time)

10:00 AM + 4 = 2:00 PM

Time at 20°E = 2:00 PM


DISTANCE CALCULATIONS USING LATITUDE & LONGITUDE

Rule:

i. 1° of latitude = 111 km (always constant)

ii. 1° of longitude varies (maximum at equator, zero at poles)


Distance by Latitude

Distance = Difference in latitude × 111 km


Example 1: Distance between 10°N and 25°N:

Difference = 25° – 10° = 15°

Distance = 15 × 111 = 1665 km

Distance = 1665 km


Example 2: Finding longitude from time If Local Time = 8:00 AM and Time at Greenwich = 6:00 AM

Difference = 2 hours

Longitude = 2 × 15 = 30°

Since local time is ahead, location is 30°E.


Example 3: If a city is 3 hours behind GMT, find the longitude.

3 × 15 = 45°

Behind GMT → West

Longitude = 45°W


Conclusion

Latitude and longitude form the backbone of geographical location systems. Latitude influences climate, while longitude affects time. Together, they provide a precise way to locate any place on Earth through coordinates. Understanding them is essential in geography, navigation, travel, mapping, global communication, and modern technologies like GPS.
